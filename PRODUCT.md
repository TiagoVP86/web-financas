# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Independent users, each managing their own personal finances. They log in on their own account, track income, expenses, recurring bills, and review AI-categorized bank statements. Context: desktop most of the time, mobile when reviewing a quick balance or adding a transaction on the go. They are financially aware but not finance professionals — they want control, not complexity.

## Product Purpose

Web Finanças lets individuals take clear ownership of their money. Track transactions (manual or via bank statement upload), set recurring bills, review spending by category, and use AI to analyze statements automatically. Success means a user can open the dashboard and immediately understand where they stand — no ambiguity, no noise.

## Brand Personality

Refined, precise, trustworthy. Quiet confidence: the kind of tool that doesn't need to show off. It earns trust through consistency, clarity, and a restrained visual language that gets out of the way of the numbers.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS**: cream backgrounds, hero-metric cards (big number + small label), identical icon grids, uppercase tracked eyebrow labels on every section.
- **Corporate banking apps**: navy + gold, stiff layouts, bloated with disclaimers, institutional heaviness.
- **Startup dashboards**: purple gradients, glassmorphism, glowing accent numbers, decorative sparkle effects.
- **Spreadsheet aesthetic**: grey everywhere, all-table all the time, no visual hierarchy, no whitespace.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers carry the page** — data is the hero. UI chrome steps back. Hierarchy exists to make financial information scannable, not to decorate.
2. **Restraint is a choice** — fewer colors, tighter spacing, less animation. Every visual element added must earn its place by communicating something.
3. **Consistency earns trust** — users handle money here. Predictable patterns (same interaction for same action, stable layout) build confidence that the tool is reliable.
4. **Personal scale, not enterprise** — this is one person's finances, not a company dashboard. Warmth is in the detail, not in big brand moments.
5. **Responsive without compromise** — the mobile experience is a first-class interface, not a scaled-down desktop. Key actions and data must be immediately accessible on any screen size.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text and UI components). Full dark-mode support already implemented. Reduced-motion alternative required for any animation added. Brazilian Portuguese (pt-BR) throughout.
